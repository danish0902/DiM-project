Back Exercises

Deadlifts

Description: Stand with feet hip-width apart, grip a barbell with hands shoulder-width apart, hinge at the hips and bend the knees to lower the weight to the ground, then stand back up, lifting the weight until hips and knees are fully extended.

Sets and Reps: Start with 3 sets of 5-8 reps.

Injury Prevention: Keep your back flat, engage your core throughout the movement, and lift with your legs rather than your back to prevent injury.

Pull-Ups

Description: Hang from a pull-up bar with hands shoulder-width apart, pull your body upward until your chin clears the bar, then lower yourself back down.

Sets and Reps: Aim for 3 sets of as many reps as possible (AMRAP).

Injury Prevention: Keep your shoulders down and back, engage your core for stability, and avoid swinging or kipping to lift your body.

Barbell Rows

Description: Stand with feet hip-width apart, grip a barbell with hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart, hinge at the hips, and bend the knees slightly, pull the barbell up toward your lower chest, then lower it back down.

Sets and Reps: Start with 3 sets of 8-12 reps.

Injury Prevention: Keep your back flat, engage your core throughout the movement, and avoid using momentum to lift the weight.

T-Bar Rows

Description: Stand on a platform with a T-bar attached, grip the handles with hands shoulder-width apart, hinge at the hips, and bend the knees slightly, pull the handles up toward your lower chest, then lower them back down.

Sets and Reps: Perform 3 sets of 8-12 reps.

Injury Prevention: Maintain proper posture with a neutral spine, engage your core for stability, and avoid rounding your back during the movement.

Dumbbell Rows

Description: Stand with feet hip-width apart, hold a dumbbell in each hand, hinge at the hips, and bend the knees slightly, pull the dumbbells up toward your lower chest, then lower them back down.

Sets and Reps: Aim for 3 sets of 10-12 reps per arm.

Injury Prevention: Keep your back flat, engage your core throughout the movement, and avoid using momentum to lift the weights.
