Biceps Exercises

Barbell Curls

Description: Stand with a barbell in hand, palms facing forward, curl the weight upward until your forearms are vertical, then lower it back down.

Sets and Reps: Start with 3 sets of 8-12 reps.

Injury Prevention: Maintain proper posture with a neutral spine, avoid swinging the weight, and use controlled movements to engage the biceps effectively.

Dumbbell Curls

Description: Stand with dumbbells in hand, palms facing forward, curl the weights upward until your forearms are vertical, then lower them back down.

Sets and Reps: Aim for 3 sets of 8-12 reps.

Injury Prevention: Keep your elbows close to your body, avoid using momentum to lift the weights, and use a full range of motion to engage the biceps effectively.

Hammer Curls

Description: Stand with dumbbells in hand, palms facing inward, curl the weights upward until your forearms are vertical, then lower them back down.

Sets and Reps: Perform 3 sets of 10-12 reps.

Injury Prevention: Maintain proper posture with a neutral spine, avoid swinging the weights, and use controlled movements to engage the biceps and forearms effectively.

Preacher Curls

Description: Sit at a preacher curl bench with arms resting on the pad, grip the barbell with palms facing upward, curl the weight upward until your forearms are vertical, then lower it back down.

Sets and Reps: Start with 3 sets of 8-12 reps.

Injury Prevention: Adjust the bench and grip to a comfortable position, maintain proper form throughout the movement, and avoid using momentum to lift the weight.

Concentration Curls

Description: Sit on a bench with a dumbbell in one hand, place the back of your arm against your inner thigh, curl the weight upward until your forearm touches your bicep, then lower it back down.

Sets and Reps: Aim for 3 sets of 10-12 reps per arm.

Injury Prevention: Maintain proper posture with a neutral spine, avoid swinging the weight, and focus on squeezing the bicep at the top of the movement.
