Leg Exercises

Squats

Description: Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, lower your body by bending your knees and hips until thighs are parallel to the ground, then stand back up to full extension.

Sets and Reps: Start with 3 sets of 8-12 reps.

Injury Prevention: Keep your knees aligned with your toes, engage your core and glutes throughout the movement, and avoid leaning too far forward or letting your knees collapse inward.

Lunges

Description: Stand with feet hip-width apart, step forward with one foot and lower your body until both knees are bent at 90-degree angles, then push back up to the starting position and repeat on the other side.

Sets and Reps: Perform 3 sets of 10-12 reps per leg.

Injury Prevention: Keep your front knee aligned with your toes, maintain a slight forward lean with your torso, and engage your core for stability.

Deadlifts

Description: Stand with feet hip-width apart, grip a barbell with hands shoulder-width apart, hinge at the hips and bend the knees to lower the weight to the ground, then stand back up, lifting the weight until hips and knees are fully extended.

Sets and Reps: Start with 3 sets of 5-8 reps.

Injury Prevention: Keep your back flat, engage your core throughout the movement, and lift with your legs rather than your back to prevent injury.

Leg Press

Description: Sit on a leg press machine with feet shoulder-width apart on the platform, push the weight upward by extending your legs, then lower it back down to the starting position.

Sets and Reps: Aim for 3 sets of 8-12 reps.

Injury Prevention: Keep your back flat against the pad, avoid locking your knees at the top of the movement, and use a controlled motion throughout.

Leg Extensions

Description: Sit on a leg extension machine with your legs bent at a 90-degree angle, extend your legs until they are straight, then lower them back down to the starting position.

Sets and Reps: Perform 3 sets of 10-15 reps.

Injury Prevention: Adjust the seat and pad to a comfortable position, keep your back against the pad throughout the movement, and avoid using momentum to lift the weight.
