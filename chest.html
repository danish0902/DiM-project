Chest Exercises

Bench Press

Description: Lie on a flat bench with a barbell racked above you. Grip the bar slightly wider than shoulder-width apart, lower it to your chest, then press it back up to full arm extension.

Sets and Reps: Start with 3 sets of 8-12 reps.

Injury Prevention: Maintain a stable grip on the bar, keep your back flat on the bench, and avoid arching excessively. Use a spotter for heavier lifts to prevent accidents.

Push-Ups

Description: Start in a plank position with hands shoulder-width apart, lower your body until your chest nearly touches the ground, then push back up to full arm extension.

Sets and Reps: Aim for 3 sets of as many reps as possible (AMRAP).

Injury Prevention: Maintain a neutral spine throughout the movement, engage your core, and keep your elbows tucked close to your body to reduce strain on the shoulders.

Dumbbell Flyes

Description: Lie on a flat bench holding dumbbells above your chest, with a slight bend in your elbows. Lower the weights out to the sides in a wide arc until you feel a stretch in your chest, then return to the starting position.

Sets and Reps: Perform 3 sets of 10-15 reps.

Injury Prevention: Use a controlled motion throughout the exercise, avoid locking your elbows at the top of the movement, and select appropriate weights to avoid overstretching the shoulder joint.

Incline Bench Press

Description: Lie on an incline bench with a barbell or dumbbells, pressing the weight upward at an angle. Lower the weight to your upper chest, then press it back up to full arm extension.

Sets and Reps: Start with 3 sets of 8-12 reps.

Injury Prevention: Adjust the bench angle to a comfortable incline, maintain proper form with a slight arch in the lower back, and avoid bouncing the weight off your chest.

Chest Dips

Description: Hold onto parallel bars with arms fully extended, lower your body by bending your elbows until your shoulders are below your elbows, then push back up to the starting position.

Sets and Reps: Aim for 3 sets of 6-10 reps.

Injury Prevention: Keep your shoulders down and back, avoid locking your elbows at the top, and use a controlled motion to prevent excessive strain on the shoulder joints.

Cable Crossover

Description: Stand between two cable machines with handles attached at shoulder height, pull the handles together in front of your body in a hugging motion, then return to the starting position.

Sets and Reps: Perform 3 sets of 10-12 reps.

Injury Prevention: Maintain a slight bend in your elbows throughout the movement, engage your core for stability, and avoid using excessive momentum to control the weight.

Chest Press Machine

Description: Sit on a chest press machine with your back flat against the pad, grip the handles, and push them forward until your arms are fully extended, then return to the starting position.

Sets and Reps: Start with 3 sets of 8-12 reps.

Injury Prevention: Adjust the seat and handles to a comfortable position, maintain proper posture throughout the movement, and avoid using momentum to push the weight.

Decline Bench Press

Description: Lie on a decline bench with your feet secured, grip the barbell with a slightly wider than shoulder-width grip, lower it to your lower chest, then press it back up to full arm extension.

Sets and Reps: Aim for 3 sets of 8-12 reps.

Injury Prevention: Ensure your feet are securely positioned on the bench, maintain a stable grip on the bar, and avoid arching your back excessively during the movement.

Dumbbell Pullover

Description: Lie on a flat bench holding a dumbbell above your chest with both hands, lower the weight behind your head in a controlled motion until your arms are parallel to the ground, then return to the starting position.

Sets and Reps: Perform 3 sets of 10-12 reps.

Injury Prevention: Keep a slight bend in your elbows throughout the movement, engage your core to stabilize your body, and avoid lowering the weight too far to prevent strain on the shoulder joints.

Pec Deck Machine

Description: Sit on a pec deck machine with your back against the pad, grip the handles, and bring them together in front of your chest in a controlled motion, then return to the starting position.

Sets and Reps: Start with 3 sets of 10-15 reps.

Injury Prevention: Adjust the seat and handles to a comfortable position, maintain proper posture throughout the movement, and avoid using excessive momentum to control the weight.
