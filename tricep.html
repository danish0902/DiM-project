Triceps Exercises

Tricep Dips

Description: Hold onto parallel bars, lower your body by bending your elbows until your shoulders are below your elbows, then push back up to the starting position.

Sets and Reps: Start with 3 sets of 6-10 reps.

Injury Prevention: Keep your shoulders down and back, avoid locking your elbows at the top, and use a controlled motion to prevent strain on the shoulder joints.

Tricep Pushdowns

Description: Stand in front of a cable machine with elbows at 90-degree angles, push the bar down until arms are fully extended, then return to the starting position.

Sets and Reps: Aim for 3 sets of 10-15 reps.

Injury Prevention: Maintain proper posture with a neutral spine, avoid arching the lower back, and use a full range of motion to engage the triceps effectively.

Close Grip Bench Press

Description: Lie on a flat bench with hands close together on the barbell, lower the weight to your lower chest, then press it back up to full arm extension.

Sets and Reps: Start with 3 sets of 8-12 reps.

Injury Prevention: Keep your elbows tucked close to your body, use a spotter for heavier lifts, and avoid bouncing the weight off your chest.

Skull Crushers

Description: Lie on a flat bench holding a barbell or dumbbells above your forehead, lower the weight by bending your elbows until they are at 90-degree angles, then extend your arms back to the starting position.

Sets and Reps: Perform 3 sets of 8-12 reps.

Injury Prevention: Use an EZ bar or dumbbells to reduce strain on the wrists, keep your elbows stable throughout the movement, and avoid lowering the weight too far to prevent excessive strain on the elbows.

Overhead Tricep Extension

Description: Stand or sit with a dumbbell or cable overhead, lower the weight behind your head by bending your elbows, then extend your arms back to the starting position.

Sets and Reps: Aim for 3 sets of 10-12 reps.

Injury Prevention: Keep your elbows close to your head, engage your core for stability, and avoid arching the lower back during the movement.
